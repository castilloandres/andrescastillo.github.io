By: Andres Castillo Ormaechea

The power of predictive analytics is becoming increasingly accessible to businesses and these predictions have the potential to optimize the sales funnel, improve conversion rates, lead veolocity and amplify revenue.

  • How can I know what is driving the web traffic behaviour that consumers are engaging in?
  • Why do some pages capture more attention while others have higher bounce rate?
  • How can I know which consumers to target with Facebook or display Ads?
  • How can I qualify and prioritize the leads generated by my digital marketing efforts?
  • How can I estimate what the value of one customer will be compared to another, so I know which one will be worth the cost of acquisition?

The answers of these questions depend on predictive modeling and predictive analytics by doing the following:, modeling consumer behaviour, qualifying and prioritizing leads, targeting and segmenting customers an

  • Modeling consumer behaviour.
  • Qualifying and prioritizing leads.
  • Targeting and segmenting customers.
  • Estimating customer lifetime value (CLV).

In the realm of sophisticated marketing, historical data can only take a business so far. Equipped with insights into customer patterns and campaign performance, the next step is to translate this data into efficient action combining marketing with predictive technologies allows marketing professionals to craft a richer picture of their audience and predict their future behaviour.

Predictive analytics is a branch of advanced analytics that is used to formulate predictions and unknown future events with tools like:

  • Data mining.
  • Statistical Analysis.
  • Automated machine learning algorithms.

Most distinctively in the fields of big data and machine learning and data science can assist marketers in:

  • Who will be my next customer?
  • How do I convert them?
  • How can I source more of these ideal customers?

These insights enable:

  • Stronger predictions.
  • Decide which campaigns to run.
  • Provide more promising leads.

Skeptics: addressing predictive modeling and missconceptions.

  • You don't need a Phd to build a predictive model.
  • A predictive model should not be a black box.
  • Even if you know your modeling data can help, modeling either "turns or confirms" assumptions.
  • Predictive models can be implemented quickly.
  • Predictive models enhance human judgement, not replace it.
  • Consumer behaviour is also important, scientific evidence is not always proof.
  • Big data can help but processing data is far more important.
  • Predictive analytics produces insights. Turning those insights into action takes both intuition and managerial skills.
  • Predictive analytics are not only for big companies.
  • Database skills + statistical knowledge + understanding the business are key for predictive analytics.
  • A models accuracy has no correlation to business value. A model can be highly accurate but offer little value to the business. For example a model that accurately predicts customer birth days holds little or no value for companies that sell to other businesses but can be very profitable to an internet company that sells online gift card to consumers.
  • Correlation is different than causation. Ice cream that kills.
  • More data usually makes predictions better but some events can radically change the model.
  • Predictive analytics can help connect between events and behaviour.
